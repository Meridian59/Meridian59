Renaissance Spells Status:

[Draft 6: Dec 1, 97]

[link to Draft 5: Oct 28, 97]

General Notes:

  • Chant (multicaster) spells are currently a little tricky to cast. Note the following:
    • A prism must be on the ground nearby to initiate or join a chant.
    • As the chant progresses, lots of mana will be continually drained. The amount drained will be lowered a bit.
    • As each caster continues to chant, spellpower will periodically be stored up in the prism.
    • When the total spellpower exceeds the amount needed for the spell, and the number of casters equals the number of sides that the prism has, the spell will fire.
    • Known bug: If you run out of mana and therefore stop chanting, things get left in a wierd state:
      • your trance flag will be stuck on, so you won't be able to cast anything else until you log off and log back on.
      • Also, the prism still thinks you're a caster, so the spell may never fire (since you have to have exactly the right number of casters for it to work). Once this has happened, the prism is permanently messed up and you have to try again with a different prism.
  • Summoning spells (wall of whatever, reflection, evil twin, etc) don't have a common object limit. Some of the spells have an individual limit (two walls of lightning, two walls of fire) but they are independent from one another.

Hunt: Chant spell. Principal chanter says the name of a murderer while chanting. After the spell fires, the hunted player acquires an enchantment which periodically tells the principle chanter his location, and as long as the enchantment lasts (proportional to spellpower and number of the murderer's pks) the principal chanter is notified every time the hunted player changes rooms. The hunted player is notified that s/he's being hunted. If the hunted player logs, the enchantment is paused (as normal) and its remaining time is multiplied by 1+piLogoffPenaltyCounter. If the hunted player tries to hide out in a non-pk safe zone, for every second spent there that second adds to the enchantment's duration, rather than subtracting from it. If the hunted player is online but the principal chanter is not, the enchantment is paused.

Bond: Chant. Targets a non-number item. When the spell fires, the item is now semi-permanently 'bonded' to whoever is holding it at the time (if it's on the ground, it defaults to the caster, and if the item is destroyed in the interim the spell fizzles). If the player loses the item, s/he will recieve periodic notification of where it is (in a location, in a player's inventory, etc.). The item may not be placed in a vault or chest. If someone other than the item's true owner logs off with it in possession, it drops to the ground. It is never auto-disposed. Each player may have exactly one bonded item at a time.
