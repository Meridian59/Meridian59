Trinity Spells/Skills

Objectives of the design for Trinity's new spells/skills:

  • Bring Weaponcraft in line with the magical schools in terms of usefulness and number of skills.
  • Fill out Jala.
  • Increase the number of low-level spells to snag new users with easy-to-cast, low-impact but neat spells.
  • Provide at least a small amount of growth for higher-level characters in the bigger schools.

A survey of current spells.

Recommendations based on that survey

Contagion: Gives caster an infectious disease which is communicable to players and monsters through various means (offers, attacks, even close proximity). Causes a very slight stat loss (1 point, random choice of stat). When a player is first infected, there is a short period during which the disease enchantment is not visible to the player, but is highly communicable. The enchantment then shows up, the stat loss goes into effect, and the disease is still communicable but less so. There may only be a maximum of x infected players at a given time (limit and counter held in system's copy of the spell). A player may not be double-infected. Cure disease works on contagion. Note that newbies can cast this, and it's not an outlaw spell. As such, stat effects and duration must be minimal (the point is just the fun of infection... wheee!!).

    gameplay details:

    • level: 2
    • mana: 10
    • vigor: 5
    • trance: 10 seconds
    • reagents: 1 entroot berry, 1 elderberry
    • type: personal enchantment
    • increase chance: 10
    • outlaw: yes
    • teacher: Maleval
    • duration: invisible for 1-5 minutes, visible for an additional 5-25 minutes, depending on spellpower and hp.

    art needs:

    • spell icon

Seance: Caster communes with the spirit of the last murder victim, who has a chance (spellpower, karma) of informing the caster as to the location of the last murderer (but not the name). May also misinform! Only works if a revenant is currently chasing that murderer. Chance of correct information is 10-50%, depending on spellpower and caster karma. Note that the chance is low so that players must communicate to be certain of the divination. After casting, caster gains invisible enchantment (duration proportional to spellpower and karma - more accurate less frequently) which ensures that further castings will provide the same answer (to avoid player casting over and over to get the right answer).

Monster Treat: (change name) When spell is cast, caster gets a monster treat object. At that time, the nearest monster checks if they care about the treat. The chance depends on the difference in power between the monster and the player, as well as on spellpower (though it never works on supermonsters). So at 1 spellpower, the chance ranges from 1%-10%, and at 99 spellpower the chance ranges from 20%-70%. The chance is halved if the monster is currently attacking someone. If the monster wants the treat, it will chase it (or whoever is carrying it) until it reaches it, upon which time it will gobble it up and resume its prior behavior (probably attacking the caster). The treat evaporates after 1-5 minutes (spellpower). If the treat leaves the room, it evaporates. If the monster leaves the room, the treat evaporates.

    gameplay details:

    • level 2

    art needs:

    • spell icon
    • treat object

Inscribe: Allows players to inscribe magical scrolls. Must use a blank scroll and reagents for target spell. Inscriber must have at least 75% in inscribed spell. Caster permanently loses 2% off spell for each scroll made. Scroll acquires 25-50% of caster's spellpower in target spell, depending on caster's inscribing spellpower. Only area enchantments (and possibly summoning spells) may be made in to scrolls.

Evade: When player is successfully hit, there is a chance proportional to skill ability and to damage done that attacking player will be forced to untarget the player (5%-50% chance - skillpower and agility - 1.5 times the chance for ranged attacks). This works for physical and magical attacks.

    gameplay details:

    • level 1

    art needs:

    • skill icon

Block: Reduces damage done by weapons and some monster attacks. Also increases damage done to weapon used to attack player, proportional to skill ability and to damage done to player. If extra damage is done to attacker's weapon, blocker's shield takes 25%-75% (skillpower, might) of that amount as well.

    gameplay details:

    • level 1

    art needs:

    • skill icon

Counterstrike: Increases damage done by player following a striking physical blow by the player's target. Damage bonus is proportional to skill ability and to the damage done by the attacker. A missing blow by the player voids the effect for the next attack.

    gameplay details:

    • level 2

    art needs:

    • skill icon

Disarm: A successful blow by the player has a chance of unequipping the target's weapon. The chance is proportional to skill ability and inversely proportional to the damage just done. A critical hit (very low damage) may even force the target to drop their weapon. Inflicting very high damage increases wear on the player's weapon. Note that this skill has a greater chance of advancing with low-damage blows.

    gameplay details:

    • level 3

    art needs:

    • skill icon

Bloodlust: When the warrior drops to 1/3 of his/her hp or 10 hp, whichever is smaller, s/he goes into a rage in which all physical attacks temporarily receive a damage multiplier (dependent on skill and stamina) of up to double, after which the player begins losing hp unless s/he rests for a period proportional to the berserk period (proportional to skill and stamina). The skill only does an advancement check when it kicks in, so if you want to be strong in this, better live on the edge!

    gameplay details:

    • level 4

    art needs:

    • skill icon

Fasting: Alters the vigor rate regained by the player by resting, depending on skill and the length of time since the player last ate. Right after eating, the vigor regaining rate is much less than normal (almost zero). After a while, it is the same. After a while more, it peaks at a value higher than normal, then it drops quickly back down to almost zero. The height of the peak increases with skill. The timescale involved also increases with skill and stamina. The skill checks for advancement every time the player eats. The chance for advancement is very sharply peaked around the vigor regaining rate peak. To advance in this skill, a warrior must thus time his/her eating carefully, gradually stretching the fast to longer and longer periods of time.

    gameplay details:

    • level 5

    art needs:

    • skill icon

Quicksilver Tracery: A special brand of Kocatani bladework which allows the warrior to recover from a missing blow more quickly, turning it into a followup attack and thus increasing his/her attack rate by 125-200% (scaled by skill and agility). Note that this skill only checks for advancement on a missing blow followed by a hit, so advancement for experienced players should be difficult.

    gameplay details:

    • level 5

    art needs:

    • skill icon

Second Wind: When the player's vigor drops to 20, s/he gets a temporary boost to 40-80 vigor (scaled by stamina) and a 'vigor debt' enchantment. After a period of time proportional to the player's skill and stamina, vigor begins being drained at a regular rate until the vigor debt is 'paid'. The skill only checks for advancement when it kicks in, which should ensure that it advances very slowly.

    gameplay details:

    • level 6

    art needs:

    • skill icon
